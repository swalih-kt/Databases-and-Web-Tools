About

HLCSdb is a comprehensive resource that encompasses genetic variants associated with the HLCS (holocarboxylase synthetase) gene, which plays a critical role in multiple metabolic processes. Mutations in this gene are known to cause Holocarboxylase Synthetase Deficiency (HCSD), a rare autosomal recessive disorder characterized by multiple carboxylase deficiency. The resource comprises 1,131 variants, meticulously curated from published scientific literature and open-access genomic databases. Each variant has been systematically annotated and classified based on the ACMG & AMP (American College of Medical Genetics and Genomics and the Association for Molecular Pathology) guidelines to ensure clinical relevance and interpretability. HLCSdb aims to serve as a valuable resource for clinicians, geneticists, and researchers, offering insights into the molecular basis of HLCS-related disorders. By providing structured, clinically actionable variant data, this database supports accurate diagnosis, facilitates genotype-phenotype correlation studies, and contributes to the development of personalized therapeutic strategies for patients affected by HLCS-associated conditions.
